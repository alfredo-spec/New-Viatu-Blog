FAQ Accordion

Two variants for the Great Migration blog post FAQ section.

Variant A — Minimal Lines

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions

The Serengeti (Tanzania) makes up the majority of the Great Migration's route, covering a much larger area. The herds spend most of the year here, especially during calving season (January to March). The Masai Mara (Kenya) is where they move between July and October during the dry season. The famous Mara River crossings happen in this section.

First decide which stage of the migration you want to see. For calving season, visit the southern Serengeti (January to March). For dramatic river crossings, head to the Mara River (July to October). Book accommodations well in advance, especially for peak seasons.

Wildebeest migrate in search of fresh grass and water, driven by seasonal rains that change resource availability across the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem. This movement is an ongoing, circular journey that keeps them moving throughout the year.

It depends on what you want to see. Calving season runs February to March in the southern Serengeti. The legendary Mara River crossings happen July to October. The herds are always moving, so there's something to witness year-round.
